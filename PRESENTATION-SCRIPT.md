# Nate Zucker — Capstone Presentation Script

**Time:** 5:00 (300s) | **Audience:** Music industry execs + Clive Davis professors

Read aloud, not memorized. Conversational beats over scripted lines.

---

## Slide 1 — Title (25s)

> "Hi, I'm Nate Zucker. I'm a multimedia designer and creative technologist —
> I make things at the intersection of music, image, code, and physical
> space. The next five minutes are a tour through some of that work."

*(Stay on this slide briefly. Smile. Move.)*

---

## Slide 2 — What I do (45s)

> "Six categories I work in. Product and UX design — apps and interaction
> systems. AI creative systems — generative workflows, real-time tools.
> Film and video. Branding and identity. Music production. And new media
> art — physical, sculptural, projection-based work.
>
> The slideshow on the left is a sampling of about a hundred things I've
> made over the last two years across all six. Today I'll show you four."

*(Land the categories quickly. Don't read each one — let the slide do that.)*

---

## Slide 3 — For Pixi (75s, hero, video plays with audio)

**Cue video to play immediately on slide enter.**

**0:00–0:15 — Voice over the intro frames:**

> "This is For Pixi — an AI-animated short I made at the MIT AI Filmmaking
> Hackathon last February. Three-day build, small team, won Best Character.
> Since then it's screened at SXSW, Cinequest, Beijing International, and
> was featured at CVPR."

**0:15–0:60 — Stop talking. Let the film play with audio.**

**0:60–0:75 — Voice as it winds down:**

> "I did creative direction and visual design on this. The thing that
> might matter most to a room like this — I also composed the score using
> AI MIDI tools. Treating the model as a compositional collaborator, not a
> replacement. Happy to talk about that workflow with anyone after."

---

## Slide 4 — Model (30s, video plays muted)

> "This is Model. It's a song from my own album, *Stem*. It's also the
> music video — I wrote the song, produced the track, directed the video,
> and built the system you see behind me. Real-time motion-tracking driving
> projected visuals. So three roles in one piece — songwriter, producer,
> creative technologist."

---

## Slide 5 — Parent and Child (30s)

> "Switching mediums. Parent and Child is a physical sculpture I built
> from custom-fabricated mycelium tiles — fungus-grown bio-material —
> paired with projection-mapped wall text. It lived as an installation.
> I show it because most of what I make is screen-based, and I want to be
> clear that I work in physical space too: with biology, with light, with
> projection."

---

## Slide 6 — Orion (25s, video plays muted)

> "Orion is a routing app. Personalization emerges from your exploration
> patterns rather than being typed in. Quests instead of points. The app
> suggests going off-track. Social identity is shaped by where you go, not
> what you say. It's a bet about where consumer software could move next."

---

## Slide 7 — Selected Work (40s, all tiles auto-play silently)

> "And there's more. *(Brief pause as the wall fills.)* This is my song
> Hearts. I did sound design for John Fogerty's tour — Radio City, the
> Beacon, 48 cities. Directed and edited Kiss for Gabby Start, Super
> Special Outing for Ella Woolsey. Made commercial work for Eataly. I'm
> not a one-medium person — that's the through-line."

*(Pace casually. The grid does the work. Don't list every credit; let the videos play.)*

---

## Slide 8 — Close (30s)

> "After Tisch I'm joining Google Creative Lab this summer as a Creative.
> Until then — I'd love to meet anyone working at the edges of creative
> technology, AI in music, new media, or just building strange things.
> The QR code goes to my site. Find me at the reception. Thank you."

*(Hold for applause. Stay on this slide.)*

## Pacing notes

- **Pixi is the load-bearing slide.** Don't rush the silent middle.
- **Don't read what's already on screen.** Captions and titles do that work.
- **Hands are quiet.** No clicker fidgeting, no pacing.
- **The Google line lands stronger if you say it once, near the end, not earlier.** Resist the urge to reveal it during Slide 1.
- **If you go over by 15s, drop Orion (Slide 6).** It's the most expendable.
- **If you go under by 15s, sit longer on Selected Work** — let the grid breathe.

## Connection-making cues

People will likely come up to ask about:
- Pixi's AI scoring workflow → have a 30-second answer ready
- The Fogerty tour → have a 30-second answer ready
- Google Creative Lab → 15-second answer (start date, what you'll do)
- "How did you build the mycelium" → 30-second answer

Have those four bullets in your head before you walk in.
